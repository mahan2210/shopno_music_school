Where your musical dream comes true

Shopno means "dream" in Bengali. At our home studio in the Winona area, we teach a unique program that blends Eastern and Western music - Bengali and Western voice, harmonium, piano, and guitar - open to every age group.

01 Classes · open for all age groups

One program, two musical worlds

Every lesson is one-on-one and shaped around the student - their learning style, previous experience, and musical goals.

  • Voice - Bengali & South Asian

    কণ্ঠসংগীত

    Bengali-focused South Asian vocal training rooted in the classical tradition - from sargam and taal to Rabindra Sangeet, Nazrul Geeti, and beyond.

  • Voice - Western

    Western vocal technique with structured progression, including preparation for Royal Conservatory of Music (RCM) voice examinations.

  • Harmonium

    হারমোনিয়াম

    The heart of South Asian song accompaniment - learn to play, and to accompany your own singing.

  • Piano

    Foundations through to graded repertoire, taught with patience for beginners of any age.

  • Guitar

    Acoustic guitar spanning both Bengali/South Asian and Western styles - chords, strumming, and song accompaniment.

  • Bengali language

    বাংলা ভাষা

    Bengali language lessons woven directly into our musical program, so young learners connect with the words they sing.

Medium of instruction: English. Lessons are available in-person at our home studio or online.

02 Team members

A family that teaches together

Founder & Lead Instructor

Dr. Bipasha Bose

An engineer by profession with a deep passion for music, Dr. Bose graduated in 1995 - the youngest student at the time - from Chhayanaut Sangeet Vidyatan, one of Bangladesh's most prestigious music schools. After relocating to Canada she remained active in music, organizing, conducting, and training for cultural programs across the community. In 2016 she founded the musical group SHOPNO to promote Bengali music among children, hosting numerous events that give both children and adults a platform to perform.

Guitar Instructor

Mr. Mrinmoy Kanungo

Also an engineer by profession, Mr. Kanungo is a dedicated guitarist with a passion for both Bengali/South Asian and Western music. He actively hosts and organizes musical events in the community, and brings a philosophy of continuous growth to his teaching - always striving to improve and evolve as a musician.

Student Teacher - Voice & Piano

Trisha

A dedicated student and enthusiastic music lover, Trisha completed her RCM Level 5 Voice examination in 2024 with First Class Honours with Distinction. Her talent in Western voice and piano is matched by her generosity - she shares her knowledge and passion by assisting as a student teacher.

Student Teacher - Junior Mentor

Trina

Our youngest team member is a budding musician with a diverse skill set across singing, piano, and guitar. Trina brings positive encouragement to our littlest learners, serving as a helping hand and fostering a warm, supportive environment.

Grow into teaching. Exceptional students are given opportunities to serve as student teachers - developing their teaching skills while deepening their own musicianship.

06 Frequently asked questions

Have questions?

Do I need to bring my own instrument?

For piano and harmonium, you can use our instruments during the lesson. For guitar, we highly recommend bringing your own so you can practice effectively at home.

Do you offer online classes?

Yes, we offer online classes for students who cannot easily attend in person, though we highly recommend in-person lessons for beginners.

What is the minimum age to start?

We typically welcome students starting at age 5, but we evaluate each child's readiness on an individual basis during the free trial class.

Are there any hidden fees?

No, there are no registration or cancellation fees. You only pay for the lessons you take, starting from your second class.
